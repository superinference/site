## Why a blog?

The SuperInference project has been communicating primarily through academic papers and documentation pages. A blog gives us a space for less formal updates: benchmark analyses, technical explorations, and engineering write-ups that do not fit neatly into a paper or a docs section.

## What to expect

### Research deep-dives

Detailed write-ups on the SuperInference framework — the PRE loop, critic-gated memory, information-theoretic stopping criteria, and the POMDP formalism that ties them together. We will cover both the theory and its practical implications for building reliable AI agents.

### Benchmark results

As we submit to new benchmarks, we will share the methodology and results here. Recent highlights include:

- **SWE-bench Live Lite**: #1 with 63.0% resolved (189/300 issues)
- **SWE-bench Live Rust**: #1 with 48.9% resolved (46/94 issues)
- **SWE-bench Live TS/JS**: #1 with 54.9% resolved (112/204 issues)
- **SWE-bench Live Go**: #1 with 74.6% resolved (103/138 issues)

### Engineering notes

How we build AMI — architecture decisions, OpenShell containerization, FRITO cost optimization, security model design, and the challenges of making an autonomous agent reliable enough to trust with real codebases.

## Stay tuned

More posts coming soon. Follow the project on [GitHub](https://github.com/superinference) for real-time updates.
